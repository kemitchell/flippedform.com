Flipped Form

A flipped form uses everyday langauge, not legalese, to express legal terms like contract obligations or policy requirements.

With a regular legal form, the lawyer is the expert. Users of the form rely on lawyers to translate their needs into legalese.

With a flipped form, users are the experts. Lawyers rely on users to express their needs in words that come natural to them.

If langauge in a flipped form doesn’t make sense to you, that’s the form’s fault, not yours. The form should change so those who use it can also understand it.
